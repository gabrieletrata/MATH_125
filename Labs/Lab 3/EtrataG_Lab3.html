Exercise 5:

cor(grad.rate.4,grad.rate.6)
## [1] 0.8821616
plot(main = "Graduation Rate in 4 Years vs. 6 Years", grad.rate.4, grad.rate.6, xlab = "Graduation Rate in 4 Years", ylab = "Graduation Rate in 6 Years")
lm(grad.rate.6~grad.rate.4)
## 
## Call:
## lm(formula = grad.rate.6 ~ grad.rate.4)
## 
## Coefficients:
## (Intercept)  grad.rate.4  
##     37.3714       0.6254
abline(37.3714, 0.6254, col="purple")

(0.8821616)^2
## [1] 0.7782091
37.3714 + (0.6254*70)
## [1] 81.1494
77-81.1494
## [1] -4.1494

  1. Observations: The relationship between the two graduation rates appears to be positive and strong.

  2. Linear model for this relationship: \[y = 37.3714 + (0.6254)x\]

  3. The line appears to fit the data well.

  4. \[R^2 = 0.7782091\]

  5. Ithaca University’s predicted six-year graduation rate is ~81%. The residual has a value of -4.1494, so the college graduates more students after six years than predicted by the four year graduation rate.

cor(sat.math.Q1, percent.admitted)
## [1] -0.6834637
plot(sat.math.Q1, percent.admitted, main = "SAT Math Score vs. Percent Admitted", xlab = "SAT Math Score", ylab = "Percent Admitted")
lm(percent.admitted~sat.math.Q1)
## 
## Call:
## lm(formula = percent.admitted ~ sat.math.Q1)
## 
## Coefficients:
## (Intercept)  sat.math.Q1  
##     154.794       -0.193
abline(154.794, -0.193, col = "red")

(-0.6834637)^2
## [1] 0.4671226
154.794 + (-0.193*600)
## [1] 38.994
39-38.994
## [1] 0.006

  1. Observations: The relationship between the two variables is strong and negative, suggesting that the more selective a school is, the higher their SAT Math score will be.

  2. Linear model for this relationship: \[y = 154.794 + (-0.193)x\]

  3. The line appears to fit the data well.

  4. \[R^2 = 0.4671226\]

  5. Stony Brook University’s predicted selectivity is ~39%. The residual has a value of 0.006, so this college is more selective than predicted by it’s SAT Math scores.

  2. Linear model for this relationship: \[y = 62.6221 + (0.3372)x\]

  3. The line does not appear to fit the data very well. This may be due to a large number of residuals under and above the line.

  4. \[R^2 = 0.299949\]

  5. SUNY Purchase’s predicted percentage of students receiving financial aid is ~63%. The residual has a value of 20.26662, so more students receive financial aid than predicted by the model.
